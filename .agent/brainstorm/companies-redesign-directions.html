Companies — three directions for a real app surface

Each is a different answer to “what is this surface for,” not just a different skin. They’re not mutually exclusive — the likely end-state blends them (a daily action view + a real per-company workspace + a coverage/prospects mode). The point of this page is to tell me which emphasis is primary. Wireframe fidelity on purpose — we’re choosing structure, not pixels. Real company data from your vault; the Fetch jobs action runs the scrape→structure→align pipeline.

Today’s baseline (what we’re replacing): a 5-column row grid + a cramped sidebar detail — a faithful Airtable clone. Because all 179 companies are active and none have ever been checked, “due for a check” returns all 179, undifferentiated — the primary daily question answers “everyone.” There is no create, no remove, no fetch, no roles-found, and the by-domain group miscounts (136 of 179 are multi-domain).

Think “Today,” scoped to companies. The verb is fetch. Bulk-fetch the due set; re-fetch ages back into the queue. Everything else (all / domain / prospects) is a tab.

Strong for

  • “What do I do right now” — the daily driver
  • Makes the pipeline the center of gravity
  • Bulk + per-company fetch; re-fetch cadence is visible

Weak for

  • Scanning the whole portfolio / comparing companies
  • Coverage-gap and “best prospects” thinking (secondary tabs)
  • Deep single-company work still needs a detail view

Coverage view re-groups the same cards by domain (multi-domain aware — a company appears under each of its domains). Detail opens as a full page, not a cramped rail.

Strong for

  • “Where should I invest research” — best prospects at a glance
  • Coverage gaps by domain (done right)
  • Logos + at-a-glance signal; feels like a CRM, not a spreadsheet

Weak for

  • Mostly dead until jobs exist (today: 179 identical “0 roles” cards)
  • Cards waste space for a 179-row list you scan by name
  • Less obvious where the daily “fetch” verb lives

The list is just navigation; the value is the workspace. This is the literal answer to “looking at a single company, I click to fetch jobs.” Add/remove live in the rail + a workspace action.

Strong for

  • Deep single-company work — fetch, review roles, read/act
  • Closest to your stated mental model (“click to fetch on a company”)
  • Room for roles + contacts + alignment + activity without cramping

Weak for

  • No strong “what needs me across all companies today” answer
  • Coverage / best-prospects need a separate mode
  • Closest structurally to the current grid+detail (risk of feeling familiar)

My read: the daily reality is A (a fetch queue is the verb you repeat), the deep reality is C (a company is a workspace, not a row), and B is a mode that only earns its keep once jobs exist. I’d lean toward A as the home + C as the detail, with B’s coverage/prospects as switchable views — but that’s exactly the call I want from you. Tell me which is primary, or describe a blend.
